## Section 1: Percentages

### Definition
Percentage means "per hundred" and expresses a fraction as parts out of 100.

### Core Formula
\[
\% = \frac{\text{Part}}{\text{Whole}} \times 100
\]

### Key Formulas
- Percentage increase: \(\frac{\text{New} - \text{Old}}{\text{Old}} \times 100\)
- Percentage decrease: \(\frac{\text{Old} - \text{New}}{\text{Old}} \times 100\)
- If A is x% more than B: \(B = \frac{A}{1 + \frac{x}{100}}\)
- If A is x% less than B: \(B = \frac{A}{1 - \frac{x}{100}}\)

### Useful Tricks
- 10% = divide by 10
- 20% = divide by 5
- 25% = divide by 4
- 50% = divide by 2
- 1% = divide by 100

### Solved Example 1
**Question:** What is 25% of 200?

**Solution:**
\[
25\% \times 200 = \frac{25}{100} \times 200 = 50
\]

### Solved Example 2
**Question:** A student's score increased from 60 to 75. What is the percentage increase?

**Solution:**
\[
\text{Increase} = 75 - 60 = 15
\]
\[
\% \text{ increase} = \frac{15}{60} \times 100 = 25\%
\]

### Interview Questions and Answers
**Q1. If price increases by 20%, by what percentage should consumption decrease to keep expenditure same?**  
Let old price = 100, new price = 120. To keep expenditure same: \(100 \times \text{old consumption} = 120 \times \text{new consumption}\) → new = \(100/120 = 5/6\). Decrease = \(1 - 5/6 = 1/6 ≈ 16.67\%\)

**Q2. A number is increased by 10% then decreased by 10%. What is the net change?**  
Net change = \(-1\%\) decrease. Formula: net = \(-\frac{x^2}{100}\) for equal +x% and -x%.

## Section 2: Profit and Loss

### Definition
- Cost Price (CP): price at which an item is bought.
- Selling Price (SP): price at which an item is sold.
- Profit = SP - CP (when SP > CP)
- Loss = CP - SP (when CP > SP)

### Core Formulas
\[
\text{Profit} = SP - CP
\]
\[
\text{Loss} = CP - SP
\]
\[
\text{Profit \%} = \frac{\text{Profit}}{CP} \times 100
\]
\[
\text{Loss \%} = \frac{\text{Loss}}{CP} \times 100
\]
\[
SP = CP \times \left(1 + \frac{\text{Profit \%}}{100}\right)
\]
\[
SP = CP \times \left(1 - \frac{\text{Loss \%}}{100}\right)
\]

### Useful Tricks
- Profit/Loss % is always calculated on CP unless stated otherwise.
- If SP of x items = CP of y items, profit/loss % = \(\frac{y - x}{x} \times 100\)
- Two successive profits of x% and y%: net = \(x + y + \frac{xy}{100}\)
- Two equal percentages +x% and -x%: net = \(-\frac{x^2}{100}\)

### Solved Example 1
**Question:** CP = Rs. 800, SP = Rs. 960. Find profit %.

**Solution:**
\[
\text{Profit} = 960 - 800 = 160
\]
\[
\text{Profit \%} = \frac{160}{800} \times 100 = 20\%
\]

### Solved Example 2
**Question:** A sells a car for Rs. 500,000 at 20% loss. Find CP.

**Solution:**
\[
SP = CP \times (1 - 0.20) = 0.8 \times CP
\]
\[
CP = \frac{500000}{0.8} = 625000
\]

### Interview Questions and Answers
**Q1. If SP of 40 articles = CP of 50 articles, find gain/loss %.**  
Let CP of 1 article = 1. CP of 50 = 50. SP of 40 = 50 → SP of 1 = 1.25. Profit = 0.25 → 25% gain.

**Q2. A man sells two cycles for Rs. 4000 each, gaining 20% on one and losing 20% on the other. Net result?**  
Net = \(-\frac{20^2}{100} = -4\%\) loss.

## Section 3: Ratio and Proportion

### Definition
- Ratio compares two quantities: \(a:b = \frac{a}{b}\)
- Proportion states equality of two ratios: \(a:b = c:d\)

### Core Formulas
\[
a:b = c:d \implies ad = bc
\]
\[
\text{If } a:b = m:n, \text{ then } a = \frac{m}{m+n} \times \text{total}, \text{ } b = \frac{n}{m+n} \times \text{total}
\]

### Useful Tricks
- Ratio simplification: divide both by GCD.
- To divide amount in ratio a:b:c: each part = \(\frac{\text{ratio}}{\text{sum of ratios}} \times \text{total}\)
- Age problems: use ratio with time offset.

### Solved Example 1
**Question:** Ratio of boys to girls is 3:2. Total students = 100. Find numbers.

**Solution:**
Sum of ratio = 5. Boys = \(\frac{3}{5} \times 100 = 60\). Girls = \(\frac{2}{5} \times 100 = 40\).

### Solved Example 2
**Question:** If \(a:b = 2:3\) and \(b:c = 4:5\), find \(a:b:c\).

**Solution:**
Make b same: \(a:b = 8:12\), \(b:c = 12:15\). So \(a:b:c = 8:12:15\).

### Interview Questions and Answers
**Q1. Rs. 6000 divided in ratio 2:3:5. Find middle share.**  
Sum = 10. Middle = \(\frac{3}{10} \times 6000 = 1800\).

**Q2. Ratio of ages is 4:5. After 10 years, ratio becomes 6:7. Find present ages.**  
Let ages = 4x, 5x. After 10 years: \(\frac{4x+10}{5x+10} = \frac{6}{7}\). Solve: 28x + 70 = 30x + 60 → 2x = 10 → x = 5. Ages = 20, 25.

## Section 4: Time and Work

### Definition
Time and Work problems involve calculating how long tasks take given worker efficiencies.

### Core Formulas
- Work = Rate × Time
- If A can do work in n days, A's 1 day work = \(\frac{1}{n}\)
- If A and B together finish in n days: \(\frac{1}{A} + \frac{1}{B} = \frac{1}{n}\)
- Man-days: Work = Men × Days

### Useful Tricks
- If A takes x days, B takes y days: together = \(\frac{xy}{x+y}\)
- Efficiency ratio: if A is twice as efficient as B, A takes half time.
- Work left = 1 - (work done)

### Solved Example 1
**Question:** A can do work in 10 days, B in 15 days. Together?

**Solution:**
A's 1 day = 1/10, B's 1 day = 1/15. Together = \(\frac{1}{10} + \frac{1}{15} = \frac{3+2}{30} = \frac{5}{30} = \frac{1}{6}\). Together = 6 days.

### Solved Example 2
**Question:** 10 men can do work in 12 days. How many days for 15 men?

**Solution:**
Work = 10 × 12 = 120 man-days. Days = \(\frac{120}{15} = 8\) days.

### Interview Questions and Answers
**Q1. A and B together finish in 6 days. A alone in 10 days. B alone?**  
\(\frac{1}{A} + \frac{1}{B} = \frac{1}{6}\). \(\frac{1}{10} + \frac{1}{B} = \frac{1}{6}\) → \(\frac{1}{B} = \frac{1}{6} - \frac{1}{10} = \frac{5-3}{30} = \frac{2}{30}\) → B = 15 days.

**Q2. 5 men + 3 boys = 10 days. 3 men + 5 boys = 12 days. Find 1 man + 1 boy together?**  
Let 1 man = m, 1 boy = b. 5m + 3b = 1/10, 3m + 5b = 1/12. Solve to get m+b = 1/20 → 20 days.

## Section 5: Time, Speed and Distance

### Definition
Speed = Distance / Time. Used for motion problems.

### Core Formulas
\[
\text{Speed} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}}
\]
\[
\text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time}
\]
\[
\text{Time} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}}
\]
- Unit conversion: km/h to m/s = × \(\frac{5}{18}\); m/s to km/h = × \(\frac{18}{5}\)
- Relative speed: same direction = \(S1 - S2\), opposite = \(S1 + S2\)

### Useful Tricks
- Average speed for equal distances: \(\frac{2S1 \times S2}{S1 + S2}\)
- If speed increases by x%, time decreases by \(\frac{x}{100+x}\)
- Train crossing pole: distance = length of train. Train crossing platform: distance = train + platform.

### Solved Example 1
**Question:** Car travels 120 km in 2 hours. Find speed.

**Solution:**
\[
\text{Speed} = \frac{120}{2} = 60 \text{ km/h}
\]

### Solved Example 2
**Question:** Train length 100 m, speed 72 km/h. Time to cross pole?

**Solution:**
Speed = \(72 \times \frac{5}{18} = 20 \text{ m/s}\). Time = \(\frac{100}{20} = 5 \text{ s}\).

### Interview Questions and Answers
**Q1. A covers same distance at 60 km/h and 40 km/h. Find average speed.**  
Average = \(\frac{2 \times 60 \times 40}{60 + 40} = \frac{4800}{100} = 48 \text{ km/h}\).

**Q2. Two trains 100 m and 150 m, speeds 60 km/h and 40 km/h opposite. Time to cross?**  
Relative speed = 100 km/h = \(100 \times \frac{5}{18} = 27.78 \text{ m/s}\). Distance = 250 m. Time = \(\frac{250}{27.78} ≈ 9 \text{ s}\).

## Section 6: Probability

### Definition
Probability = Favorable outcomes / Total outcomes.

### Core Formulas
\[
P(E) = \frac{\text{Favorable outcomes}}{\text{Total outcomes}}
\]
\[
P(\text{not } E) = 1 - P(E)
\]
\[
P(A \text{ or } B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \text{ and } B)
\]
\[
P(A \text{ and } B) = P(A) \times P(B) \text{ (independent)}
\]

### Useful Tricks
- For coin: total outcomes = \(2^n\) for n coins.
- For dice: total outcomes = \(6^n\) for n dice.
- Combinations: use C(n,k) for selecting without order.

### Solved Example 1
**Question:** One die rolled. Probability of even number?

**Solution:**
Even = {2, 4, 6} = 3 outcomes. Total = 6. \(P = \frac{3}{6} = 0.5\).

### Solved Example 2
**Question:** Two coins tossed. Probability of exactly one head?

**Solution:**
Outcomes = {HH, HT, TH, TT} = 4. Exactly one head = {HT, TH} = 2. \(P = \frac{2}{4} = 0.5\).

### Interview Questions and Answers
**Q1. Bag has 3 red, 2 blue. 2 balls drawn. Probability both red?**  
Total = 5. Ways to choose 2 = C(5,2) = 10. Both red = C(3,2) = 3. \(P = \frac{3}{10} = 0.3\).

**Q2. Die rolled twice. Probability sum = 7?**  
Pairs = (1,6), (2,5), (3,4), (4,3), (5,2), (6,1) = 6. Total = 36. \(P = \frac{6}{36} = \frac{1}{6}\).

## Section 7: Permutations and Combinations

### Definition
- Permutation: arrangement with order (P(n,k))
- Combination: selection without order (C(n,k))

### Core Formulas
\[
P(n,k) = \frac{n!}{(n-k)!}
\]
\[
C(n,k) = \frac{n!}{k!(n-k)!}
\]
\[
n! = n \times (n-1) \times \dots \times 1
\]

### Useful Tricks
- Permutation of n items: n!
- With repetition: \(n^k\) for k positions.
- C(n,k) = C(n, n-k)
- For words: arrange letters, consider repeated letters.

### Solved Example 1
**Question:** Arrange 4 books. How many ways?

**Solution:**
4! = 24 ways.

### Solved Example 2
**Question:** Choose 3 from 5. How many ways?

**Solution:**
C(5,3) = \(\frac{5!}{3!2!} = \frac{120}{6 \times 2} = 10\).

### Interview Questions and Answers
**Q1. How many 3-digit numbers from 1,2,3,4 without repetition?**  
P(4,3) = \(\frac{4!}{1!} = 24\).

**Q2. Word "APPLE". Arrangements?**  
5 letters, P repeated twice. Arrangements = \(\frac{5!}{2!} = 60\).

## Section 8: Simple and Compound Interest

### Definition
- Simple Interest (SI): interest on principal only.
- Compound Interest (CI): interest on principal + accumulated interest.

### Core Formulas
\[
SI = \frac{P \times R \times T}{100}
\]
\[
A = P + SI = P \left(1 + \frac{R \times T}{100}\right)
\]
\[
CI = P \left(1 + \frac{R}{100}\right)^T - P
\]
\[
A = P \left(1 + \frac{R}{100}\right)^T
\]

### Useful Tricks
- For 2 years: CI - SI = \(\frac{P \times R^2}{100^2}\)
- For equal amounts: use average rate.
- Half-yearly compounding: rate = R/2, time = 2T.

### Solved Example 1
**Question:** P = Rs. 1000, R = 10%, T = 2 years. Find SI.

**Solution:**
\[
SI = \frac{1000 \times 10 \times 2}{100} = 200
\]

### Solved Example 2
**Question:** Same P, R, T. Find CI.

**Solution:**
\[
CI = 1000 \left(1 + \frac{10}{100}\right)^2 - 1000 = 1000 \times 1.21 - 1000 = 210
\]

### Interview Questions and Answers
**Q1. SI on Rs. 5000 at 8% for 3 years?**  
SI = \(\frac{5000 \times 8 \times 3}{100} = 1200\).

**Q2. CI - SI for 2 years = Rs. 40, P = 1000. Find R.**  
CI - SI = \(\frac{1000 \times R^2}{10000} = 40\) → \(R^2 = 400\) → R = 20%.

## Section 9: Averages

### Definition
Average = Sum of terms / Number of terms.

### Core Formulas
\[
\text{Average} = \frac{\text{Sum}}{\text{Count}}
\]
\[
\text{Sum} = \text{Average} \times \text{Count}
\]

### Useful Tricks
- New average after adding x: new sum = old sum + x, new count = old count + 1.
- Weighted average: \(\frac{n1 \times a1 + n2 \times a2}{n1 + n2}\)
- If average of n numbers is A and one number removed is x, new average = \(\frac{nA - x}{n-1}\)

### Solved Example 1
**Question:** Average of 5 numbers = 20. Find sum.

**Solution:**
Sum = 20 × 5 = 100.

### Solved Example 2
**Question:** Ages of 4 students = 18, 20, 22, 24. Find average.

**Solution:**
Sum = 84. Average = 84 / 4 = 21.

### Interview Questions and Answers
**Q1. Average of 6 numbers = 15. Remove 10. New average?**  
Sum = 90. New sum = 80. New average = 80 / 5 = 16.

**Q2. Class: 20 boys avg 18, 30 girls avg 22. Overall avg?**  
Weighted = \(\frac{20 \times 18 + 30 \times 22}{50} = \frac{360 + 660}{50} = 20.4\).

## Section 10: Number System

### Definition
Number system deals with types of numbers: natural, whole, integers, prime, composite, even, odd.

### Core Concepts
- Natural numbers: 1, 2, 3, ...
- Whole numbers: 0, 1, 2, 3, ...
- Integers: ..., -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, ...
- Prime: divisible only by 1 and itself (2, 3, 5, 7, ...)
- Composite: more than 2 divisors
- Even: divisible by 2
- Odd: not divisible by 2

### Useful Tricks
- Sum of first n natural numbers: \(\frac{n(n+1)}{2}\)
- Sum of first n even numbers: \(n(n+1)\)
- Sum of first n odd numbers: \(n^2\)
- Divisibility: by 2 (ends even), 3 (sum of digits multiple of 3), 5 (ends 0/5), 10 (ends 0)

### Solved Example 1
**Question:** Sum of first 10 natural numbers?

**Solution:**
\[
\frac{10 \times 11}{2} = 55
\]

### Solved Example 2
**Question:** Is 91 prime?

**Solution:**
91 = 7 × 13 → not prime.

### Interview Questions and Answers
**Q1. Smallest prime number?**  
2.

**Q2. How many primes between 1 and 20?**  
2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19 → 8 primes.

## Section 11: Logical Reasoning

### Definition
Logical reasoning tests pattern recognition, deduction, and problem-solving without relying on domain knowledge.

### Common Types
- Series completion (number, alphabet)
- Coding-decoding
- Syllogisms
- Blood relations
- Directions and distances
- Puzzles
- Seating arrangements
- Data sufficiency
- Analogies

### Useful Tricks
- Number series: look for +, -, ×, ÷, squares, cubes.
- Alphabet series: A=1, B=2, ..., Z=26.
- Coding: letter shift, reverse, position-based.
- Syllogism: use Venn diagrams.
- Direction: use north-up convention.

### Solved Example 1
**Question:** Complete series: 2, 4, 8, 16, ?

**Solution:**
Pattern: ×2 → next = 32.

### Solved Example 2
**Question:** If A is B's mother, B is C's father, C is D's brother. How is A related to D?

**Solution:**
A → B → C → D. A is D's grandmother.

### Interview Questions and Answers
**Q1. Series: 3, 6, 11, 18, 27, ?**  
Differences: 3, 5, 7, 9 → next diff = 11 → 27 + 11 = 38.

**Q2. If P is Q's brother, Q is R's sister, R is S's father. How is P related to S?**  
P → Q → R → S. P is S's uncle.

## Section 12: Data Interpretation

### Definition
Data Interpretation (DI) involves analyzing tables, charts, graphs, and extracting insights.

### Common Types
- Tables
- Bar graphs
- Pie charts
- Line graphs
- Caselets
- Mixed DI

### Useful Tricks
- Read question before data.
- Estimate quickly before exact calculation.
- Use percentages for comparisons.
- Look for trends, not just values.
- Practice speed reading of charts.

### Solved Example 1
**Question:** Bar graph shows sales: 2020 = 100, 2021 = 120. Find % increase.

**Solution:**
\[
\% = \frac{120 - 100}{100} \times 100 = 20\%
\]

### Solved Example 2
**Question:** Pie chart: total = 500. Sector = 20%. Find value.

**Solution:**
Value = 20% × 500 = 100.

### Interview Questions and Answers
**Q1. Table: A=100, B=150, C=200. Find B's % of total.**  
Total = 450. B% = \(\frac{150}{450} \times 100 = 33.33\%\)

**Q2. Line graph: 2019 = 80, 2020 = 100, 2021 = 120. Avg growth?**  
Growth: 20, 20 → avg = 20.
